in plain terms

for readers who do not trade — three questions cover it.

what is this?

a private trading operation in indian derivatives that publishes its complete results — wins, losses, and errors — as they happen. no entry is edited after the fact, and every figure is checked against the broker's own records before it appears here.

what does "R" mean?

results are measured against a fixed daily risk budget, called 1R — the most the desk allows itself to lose in one session. a +2R day earned twice that budget. measuring in R keeps the focus on discipline rather than on the size of the account.

why publish everything?

most track records show only the good parts. a book that publishes its mistakes in real time cannot quietly rewrite its history. that is the entire point — the record is the product.

the doctrine

principle i

asymmetry over win-rate

losing positions are cut small; winning positions are given room. the desk trades for positive skew — several small scratches financed by one extended run. the distribution above shows that shape.

principle ii

survival before size

a fixed breaker guards the base every session. no averaging down, no size escalation after a loss, no undefined risk. the desk's first obligation is to open tomorrow.

principle iii

evolve daily

every close produces a written rule — what failed, what was corrected, what compounds. one percent sharper each session, in writing.
